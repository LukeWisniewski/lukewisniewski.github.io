Luke Wisniewski

I am a fourth year undergraduate student at Dartmouth College double majoring in economics and mathematics. I am driven by taking quantiative information and applying it to interesting social questions. I have done research in Dartmouth Economics and Mathematics Mathematics departments, as well as the Tuck School of Business at Dartmouth. I am currently completing an internship at the ifo Institute for Economic Research in Munich, Germany in the Labor and Demographics Center.

While much of my economic research has pertained to sports economics (a great passion and hobby of mine), I am also interested in addressing more pressing social concerns such as education and the climate. My work in mathematics has involved numerical simulations of delay differential equation systems, with a particular focus on applications towards climate modeling.
